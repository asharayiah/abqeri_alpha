Framing

This is a didactic, rule-based field toy model encoding a global self-consistency constraint that qualitatively recreates delayed-choice, eraser, and entanglement-like correlations. It avoids superluminal signalling and ontic state collapse by treating correlations as selection effects over the space of allowed whole-story solutions.

Modes

  • N-Body: Newtonian-like pair potentials.
  • Retro-Slit: late binary switch modulates interference-like coupling terms.
  • Eraser: toggles coherence penalty vs which-path bias.
  • Entanglement: pairwise harmony constraints preserve joint outcomes under local reads.
  • Harmony: global relaxation toward a low-tension configuration after boundary changes.

On Bell and Einstein

Bell inequalities constrain local hidden-variable models under factorization assumptions. Our toy stresses a different primitive: boundary-driven consistency. Non-classical statistics arise from solution selection, not message-passing; locality (as “no usable FTL channel”) is respected while reproducing quantum-like patterns.
